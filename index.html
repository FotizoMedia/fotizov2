The problem

Most small-business owners are operating in the dark.

The cost isn't usually the marketing — it's the not-knowing. Tools that don't talk to each other. Reports nobody can read. Vendors who go quiet between invoices. And the constant low-grade question: is any of this even working?

“My tools don't talk to each other.”

CRM here, email there, social scheduler somewhere else, booking system in a fourth place, reviews managed manually. Six logins, six dashboards, none of them connected. Information lives in silos and customers fall through the gaps.

“My reports don't tell me anything.”

Every month brings a PDF full of numbers without context. Impressions are up; sales aren't. Bounce rate dropped; nobody can say what that means. The data is there. The insight isn't.

“I only hear from my agency at renewal.”

A campaign launched. Now what? Months go by without a proactive update — no analysis, no recommendations, no signal that anyone's watching. You're paying for partnership and getting service.

There's a different way to do this. We call it turning the lights on.

01

Illuminate.

You shouldn't have to guess whether your marketing is working. We make it visible — what's happening, what's working, what isn't, and why. Before any tactic, we turn the lights on.

02

One connected system, not seven dashboards.

03

We tell you what's coming — not what already happened.

Most agencies report what already happened. Fotizo tells you what's coming. You don't just get numbers — you get the insight and the recommendation that would normally cost you a full-time strategist to produce.

Fotizo isn't a project shop and isn't a contract-locked retainer. We work as a real partner — choose the engagement that fits where you are, and we run the work as long as the relationship is the right fit. No contracts. No black box.

What we won't do

What we won't do — and why.

Most agencies hide the rules of the engagement until you've committed. We put ours on the homepage. Four things Fotizo refuses to do — and the reasons we refuse them.

01

We won't lock you into auto-renewing contracts.

We work on agreements. If at any point we're not the right fit, you can leave amicably. We don't believe in holding clients hostage — and we don't need to, because if we're doing our job, you'll want to stay.

02

We won't hold your assets hostage.

Your domain, your CRM, your ad accounts, your analytics, your data — all in your name, all accessible to you, all available to take with you if our relationship ends. We never lock up what's yours.

03

We won't send you vanity reports.

Every report tells you what changed, what it means, and what we recommend doing next — not just a dashboard screenshot with last month's numbers. If a number doesn't lead somewhere, it doesn't belong in the report.

04

We won't sell you services you don't need.

If something we offer isn't going to move your business, we'll tell you. We'd rather earn a smaller engagement that delivers than a larger one that doesn't.

One more, plus the reasoning behind each, lives on our Why Fotizo page.

The people behind Fotizo

Two founders. One who's lived inside the agencies he didn't want to copy. One who builds the systems most agencies never get right.

Fotizo was founded by Sahen Sevilla and Tielyr Ritnour. Sahen spent over a decade inside marketing agencies — six of those years running his own — and most recently led operations at a mid-market firm working across more than 500 small-business clients. He started Fotizo because he didn't want to see another small business fail that didn't need to. Tielyr comes from the systems and IT-infrastructure side of the work — the layer that makes Fotizo's Integrate promise real instead of aspirational. Together, the company is built around what they've seen the industry do badly by design — and what an agency that actually cared about small-business outcomes would do differently.
